NAME OF GAME
NARRATIVE DESIGNER, PRODUCER | DEC 2022 - PRESENT
Point and Click Adventure. On a tiny strip of rocky beach in a sea-monster infested bay, Roe lives with his lighthouse keeper grandmother. But, one day, 12-year old Roe discovers the light is missing. Worse, there’s a monster in the bay leading sailors astray with its false light. In-development.
GOALS
  • Create thought-provoking game about humanity’s relationship with nature.
  • Develop unique steampunk, fantasy world.
  • Tell compelling coming-of-age story.
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Oversaw project timeline, goals, and milestones.
  • Scheduled weekly syncs within and across art, programmming, writing, and design teams to address potential risks.
  • Outlined main storyline, 4 characters, and narrative goals.
  • Worldbuilt steampunk world alongside 4-person writing team.
  • Developed and pitched presentation, including budget and timeline, to 3 investors.
  • Edited and reviewed 5 core story cutscenes to maintain tone and continuity.

WHAT I LEARNED
  • 1. Have the hard conversations. Making a game isn’t easy. As college seniors facing our final round of theses and capstone projects, the team struggled to produce our first slice; we re-scoped Angler Angel three times. By having the hard conversations about our commitment to the game and what we realistically could accomplish meant we eased a lot of stress and tension from the team. It allowed us to work more effectively and, more importantly, built stronger teamwork.
  • 2. Do post-mortems Understanding what went wrong was vital to making sure our future actions flowed smoothly. We worked to discuss this as a group, and I ensured each conversation was civil and productive. Editing and feedback is a vital part of completing any project, so sitting down with the team and understanding why we took each step was a great learning experience.
