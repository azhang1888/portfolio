PLAYER PROFILES
WRITER, RESEARCHER | JUN 2022 to PRESENT (HIATUS)
During my time as a paid Game Master (GM), I encountered a wide variety of players. It got me thinking—what were the general trends among their playstyles? More importantly, how could I create better (and more empowering!) worlds for them to engage with? To improve my collaborative storytelling skills and better craft narratives for my players, I began surveying them about safety tools and documenting what I’ve observed.
Read Here.
GOALS
  • Develop safer, more compelling play by implementing safety features.
  • Determine which safety features were effective and the various pros/cons while using them.
  • Improve how I gather feedback from my players and how I incorporate them.
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Solicit and incorporate feedback across 5 groups of players.
  • Worked with 3 other GMs to offer thoughtful feedback and iterate on safety tool implementation during play.
  • Researched various safety tools and developed methods to implement them.
  • Observed player reactions and table dynamics.
  • Generated 5 player profiles, anticipating what style of play they would enjoy and what risks they may pose in collaborative play.
TOOLS
  • Google Suite (Calendar, Forms, Sheets, Docs)
  • Miro
  • Discord
WHAT I LEARNED
  • 1. Defining “better play.” Specifically, what each player considers enjoyable play and what they prioritize in it. Knowing these player profiles meant I could focus on creating a story my players enjoyed. Whether it was a political thriller or a dungeon crawl, better play meant different things to each party I worked with. Knowing what they expected and what they would consider improvements was vital to improving my own work.
