WRITER | MAR 2023 to PRESENT
Interactive Fiction. Listen, it’s not easy being an Elvis impersonator in Vegas. Shotgun weddings, medicore renditions of “Hound Dog”---you’ve done it all. But, it’s even harder when you’re the only ordained minister up at 3 AM when a demon’s on the Strip. This is definitely a passion project, which I've been using to test out aspects of game writing I wanted to practice. In-development.
GOALS
  • Develop engaging puzzles and dialogue in a text-based choose your own adventure format.
  • Produce humorous and playful mystery with branching dialogue.
  • Improve my coding skills.
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Wrote barks for 2 non-player characters for worldbuilding and characterization.
  • Worldbuilt urban fantasy Las Vegas.
  • Wrote action and dialogue options in humorous tone.
  • Designed 3 text-based puzzles and 2 levels for the player to navigate.
  • Programmed game in Twine.
TOOLS
  • Scrivener
  • Miro
  • Google Suite (Excel, Calendar)
  • Twine (HTML, CSS, & JS)
WHAT I LEARNED
  • 1. Play games to make games. Interactive fiction (IF) is a medium I’m definitely familiar with, but most IF pieces I’ve made were relatively linear and had a visual component. I found myself playing more IF games, studying how each one chose to incorporate gameplay and mechanics. At the end, I realized I was adopting and iterating on more mechanics than I could’ve originally imagined.
