KNIGHT CITY
WRITER, GAME DESIGNER | JAN 2022 to AUG 2022
A tabletop setting created to test Blueprint. In Knight City, superhero defender Lancelot just died. And you're the main culprit(s) for his murder.
Play Here.
GOALS
  • Test Blueprint’s core mechanics
  • Create narrative-driven mechanics to allow for flexible and engaging gameplay.
  • Test how the Blueprint system functions in play and incorporate player feedback.
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Wrote flavor text, core rules, mechanics, character customization options, and examples.
  • Worldbuilt superhero setting and outlined potential character arcs.
  • Designed and drew format as well as original art to capture darker atmosphere.
  • Led 2 playtesting sessions to record and iterate mechanics based on player feedback.
  • Wrote one page character and world outlines to pitch campaign.
TOOLS
  • Procreate
  • Microsoft Office (Word, Excel, Forms)
  • Adobe Creative Cloud (InDesign)
WHAT I LEARNED
  • 1. Your playtesters are also your collaborators! As I began playtesting more stringently, I realized a lot of comments my (awesome) playtesters left were also ideas on how to iterate on my mechanics. Playtesting to me has always been a Google Form with a list of survey questions, a process I was incredibly familiar with from my research background, but when I brought my playtesters into the development process—I saw my games flourish in ways I didn’t expect. It was so creatively rewarding to be offered a different perspective on my games and to hear what they were interested in playing.
