SENSELESS
WRITER | SEP 2022 to PRESENT
2.5 Roleplaying Game. Noah wakes up with no memories and no senses. Yet, this new world knows him, and more than that—is afraid of him. He has to know why. In-development.
GOALS
  • Create fun action-adventure game, inspired by JRPGs.
  • Tell a story the player will remember (positively)
RESPONSIBILITIES
  • Coordinated biweekly writing team syncs via when2meet and Google Calendar.
  • Wrote barks for 3 background characters to further build world and set mysterious atmosphere.
  • Wrote engaging 5 cutscenes, balancing humor and a more serious tone.
  • Worked with programming and art team to develop environmental barks system.
  • Worldbuilt fantasy concepts into a science fiction world.
  • Scoped overarching World 2 plotline, characters, and levels.
  • Developed roadmap with key production milestones to ensure each team was updated.
  • Communicated narrative and gameplay goals across 23-person programming, art, design, and narrative teams.
  • Organized writing team sprints and sync-ups with programming, audio, and art team to communicate assets needed in a timely manner.
TOOLS
  • Unity (C#)
  • Discord
  • Google Suite (Excel, Docs, Calendar, Forms)
WHAT I LEARNED
  • 1. Over-communication is better than under-communicating In a 23-person team, it’s easy for one team to not really understand what another is doing. As a result, I realized talking about everything I did and all my concerns would be more helpful than not talking at all. It was a process that allowed us to address a lot of bugs and story issues early on, helping us connect gameplay and narrative more closely.
  • 2. One page pitches will save your life Since the team was so large, having a concise summary of what we planned to do was vital to keep tasks moving smoothly. I began preparing central documents for the entire writing team and encouraging other teams to do the same.
